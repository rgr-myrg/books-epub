HALLUCINATION

The Shadow across the hedge
  is just Light dressed in black.

To stop feeling lonely
  break a mirror.

Bells toll
  when her clitoris is clutched.

Simplicity becomes clothed
  with the flower of silence.

Use the magic needle
  to stitch the eye onto your tears.

You hammer your fingers
  into the music
    and swallows appear.

Life has its own back pain.

Heal yourself with guitars,
  with a child-safe blade
    or with the echo of a saint.

In the mirror you view your face
  from the other side.

If death is a thread,
  you sew your own riddle.

The devil spewing fire
  is a brilliant idea.

This illusion is a cobweb
  which never ends.

Sorrows coalesce into foam
  over the oceans.

To stop crying each sunrise,
  silence becomes the rooster.

Gaze at the living: They are dead.

Gaze at the dead: They are living.

You search for darkness;
  hard to touch
    yet easy to feel.

You cure the crazy ones
  with a potion of silence
    and they all
      want the remedy.

You create, paint, and draw…

And in the end,
  you know well
    that hope is a
      kind whore.
